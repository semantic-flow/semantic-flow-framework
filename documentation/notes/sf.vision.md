**Semantic meshes** will be the foundation for a new era of the semantic web, where people can easily mint their own expressive identifiers, and manage and version their own data, 

**Semantic Flow** is a framework for publishing resource identifiers (IRIs) and managing digital artifacts, especially knowledge graphs and other semantic data. By leveraging GitHub, Gitlab, and other static hosting services for storage and publishing, it enables a **dereferenceable, pseudo-immutable Semantic Web** where every IRI returns meaningful content.

[[sflo.concept.mesh-repo]] provide storage, [[Semantic Meshes|sflo.concept.mesh]] provide resource management and metadata, and [[semantic sites|sflo.concept.semantic-site]] support data discovery and explainability.

## Benefits

- own your own self-describing data
- complete version history for digital artifacts when you want it
- reliable persistence
- truly FAIR (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, and Reusable)

## Features

- seamlessly integrate other data sources anywhere in your mesh
- generate and customize mini-sites or single-page applications for nodes in your mesh
